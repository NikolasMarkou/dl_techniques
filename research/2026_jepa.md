# JEPA and Variants: Mathematical Reference

## 0. LeCun's Intuition

JEPA is LeCun's answer to a specific complaint about generative models: **predicting the input is the wrong objective**. His canonical example is video — trying to predict every pixel of future frames is nearly impossible because of chaotic details like flickering leaves or ripples on a pond. A pixel-level predictor wastes capacity modeling unpredictable high-entropy noise. A JEPA operating in representation space "can choose to ignore certain details that are not easily predictable or relevant", so the encoder learns to strip out what doesn't matter and keep only what's needed to predict the future.

Framed as information theory: the representation is a **lossy compression** of the input that retains only the structure needed to reduce uncertainty about what comes next. This is why LeCun views JEPA as aligned with predictive coding in the brain, where neurons encode surprising deviations and higher-level features rather than raw sensory detail.

The deeper architectural claim is that JEPA is an **energy-based model**, not a probabilistic generator. JEPA is not generative in the sense that it can be sampled from; it merely captures the dependencies between $x$ and $y$ without explicitly generating $y$. The energy function $F(x,y) = \left\| g_\phi(s_x, z) - s_y \right\|^2$ assigns low values to compatible pairs and high values to incompatible ones. This sidesteps the curse of dimensionality that plagues generative models in continuous, multimodal domains — instead of placing probability mass over all possible futures, you just shape an energy landscape, and latent variables $z$ carry the residual information about $y$ that cannot be extracted from $x$.

LeCun's critique of autoregressive LLMs follows directly. He considers them a conceptual dead end on the road to Advanced Machine Intelligence because token-by-token generation is System-1 reactive: fixed compute per token, no planning, no ability to reason in an abstract space before committing to output. Error compounds with sequence length because each token is sampled from surface statistics rather than from an underlying world model. JEPA is meant to be the **world-model substrate** — an abstract representation where a separate reasoning/planning module can do energy minimization (System 2) before any output is generated. The hierarchical extension **H-JEPA** stacks JEPAs across time scales so the same architecture can plan at both "contract the quadriceps" and "cross the road" levels of abstraction.

Consequences for the math below: every JEPA loss lives in embedding space, every anti-collapse mechanism exists because EBMs trained without contrastive samples otherwise flatten the energy surface, and every variant is an attempt to preserve the "predict meaning, not pixels/tokens" principle while making training stable at scale.

## 1. Base JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture)

### Setup

Given an input $x$, split it into a **context** $x_c$ and a **target** $x_t$ (e.g., masked region).

Three networks:

- Context encoder $f_\theta : x_c \mapsto s_c \in \mathbb{R}^d$
- Target encoder $f_{\bar\theta} : x_t \mapsto s_t \in \mathbb{R}^d$ (parameters $\bar\theta$ = EMA of $\theta$)
- Predictor $g_\phi : (s_c, z) \mapsto \hat{s}_t \in \mathbb{R}^d$, where $z$ is a conditioning variable (e.g., position of the target)

### Objective

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{JEPA}}(\theta, \phi) = \mathbb{E}_{x,\, (x_c, x_t)} \left\| g_\phi(f_\theta(x_c),\, z) - \mathrm{sg}\!\left[ f_{\bar\theta}(x_t) \right] \right\|_2^2
$$

where $\mathrm{sg}[\cdot]$ is the stop-gradient operator.

### EMA target update

$$
\bar\theta \leftarrow \tau\, \bar\theta + (1 - \tau)\, \theta, \qquad \tau \in [0.99,\, 0.9999]
$$

### Collapse prevention

Without regularization, the trivial solution $f_\theta \equiv f_{\bar\theta} \equiv \text{const}$ minimizes the loss. Prevented via:

1. Asymmetry: stop-gradient on target branch
2. EMA target encoder (slow-moving teacher)
3. Predictor bottleneck

Key property: **loss is computed in embedding space $\mathbb{R}^d$, not input space.** No pixel/token reconstruction.

## 2. I-JEPA (Image JEPA)

Image $x \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times W \times 3}$ tokenized into $N$ patches $\{ p_i \}_{i=1}^{N}$.

Sample one context block $B_c \subset [N]$ and $M$ target blocks $\{ B_{t,m} \}_{m=1}^{M}$ (non-overlapping).

$$
s_c = f_\theta\!\left( \{ p_i : i \in B_c \} \right), \qquad s_{t,m} = f_{\bar\theta}\!\left( \{ p_i : i \in B_{t,m} \} \right)
$$

Predictor conditioned on target block positional tokens $\mathbf{m}_{t,m}$:

$$
\hat{s}_{t,m} = g_\phi(s_c,\, \mathbf{m}_{t,m})
$$

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{I-JEPA}} = \frac{1}{M} \sum_{m=1}^{M} \left\| \hat{s}_{t,m} - \mathrm{sg}\!\left[ s_{t,m} \right] \right\|_2^2
$$

## 3. V-JEPA / V-JEPA 2 (Video JEPA)

Video $x \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times H \times W \times 3}$, tokenized into spatio-temporal patches (tubelets) indexed by $(t, i, j)$.

Masking strategy: sample tubelet masks that span time (short-range and long-range). Let $B_c, B_t$ be context and target tubelet index sets.

Same objective form:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{V-JEPA}} = \left\| g_\phi\!\left( f_\theta(x_{B_c}),\, \mathbf{m}_{B_t} \right) - \mathrm{sg}\!\left[ f_{\bar\theta}(x_{B_t}) \right] \right\|_2^2
$$

V-JEPA 2 adds action conditioning for world-model use:

$$
\hat{s}_{t+1} = g_\phi(s_t,\, a_t), \qquad \mathcal{L} = \left\| \hat{s}_{t+1} - \mathrm{sg}\!\left[ f_{\bar\theta}(x_{t+1}) \right] \right\|_2^2
$$

## 4. VL-JEPA (Vision-Language JEPA)

Two encoders:

- $X$-encoder $f_\theta^X$: visual input $v \mapsto s^X \in \mathbb{R}^d$
- $Y$-encoder $f_{\bar\theta}^Y$: text $y \mapsto s^Y \in \mathbb{R}^d$ (often frozen pretrained text model)

Predictor maps visual embedding into text embedding space:

$$
\hat{s}^Y = g_\phi(s^X)
$$

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{VL-JEPA}} = \left\| g_\phi\!\left( f_\theta^X(v) \right) - \mathrm{sg}\!\left[ f_{\bar\theta}^Y(y) \right] \right\|_2^2
$$

Key point: predicts **continuous text embeddings**, not tokens. No autoregressive decode at inference for perception tasks.

## 5. LeJEPA (Latent-Euclidean JEPA)

Removes EMA teacher, stop-gradient asymmetry, and predictor tricks. Uses a single regularizer: **SIGReg** (Sketched Isotropic Gaussian Regularization).

### Shared encoder

$$
s_c = f_\theta(x_c), \qquad s_t = f_\theta(x_t)
$$

(same $\theta$, no EMA)

### Prediction loss

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{pred}} = \left\| g_\phi(s_c,\, z) - s_t \right\|_2^2
$$

### SIGReg: force embeddings toward $\mathcal{N}(0, I_d)$

Draw $K$ random unit directions $\{ u_k \}_{k=1}^{K} \sim \mathrm{Unif}(\mathbb{S}^{d-1})$. For each, project the batch of embeddings $\{ s_i \}_{i=1}^{B}$:

$$
\pi_k = \left\{ u_k^\top s_i \right\}_{i=1}^{B}
$$

Under $s \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I_d)$, each projection $\pi_k \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1)$. Test this via a distributional distance $D$ (e.g., 1-D Wasserstein, energy distance, or Cramér-von Mises) against the standard normal CDF $\Phi$:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{SIGReg}} = \frac{1}{K} \sum_{k=1}^{K} D\!\left( \hat{F}_{\pi_k},\; \Phi \right)
$$

### Total loss

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{LeJEPA}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{pred}} + \lambda\, \mathcal{L}_{\text{SIGReg}}
$$

One hyperparameter $\lambda$. The isotropic Gaussian constraint provably prevents collapse because $\mathcal{N}(0, I_d)$ is non-degenerate in every direction.

## 6. LLM-JEPA

Combines standard next-token prediction with a JEPA term using **multiple views of the same knowledge** (e.g., natural-language problem description $x^{\text{NL}}$ and code solution $x^{\text{CODE}}$).

### Standard LM loss

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{NTP}}(\theta) = - \sum_{t=1}^{T} \log p_\theta\!\left( x_t \mid x_{<t} \right)
$$

### JEPA term

Let $h_\theta(x) \in \mathbb{R}^d$ be a pooled hidden-state representation from the LLM (e.g., last-token hidden state at a designated layer). For a paired view $(x^a, x^b)$:

$$
s^a = h_\theta(x^a), \qquad s^b = h_\theta(x^b)
$$

Predictor $g_\phi$ maps one view's embedding to the other:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{JEPA}}^{\text{LLM}} = \left\| g_\phi(s^a) - \mathrm{sg}\!\left[ s^b \right] \right\|_2^2
$$

### Combined objective

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{LLM-JEPA}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{NTP}} + \alpha\, \mathcal{L}_{\text{JEPA}}^{\text{LLM}}
$$

with mixing weight $\alpha$ (typically small, e.g., $0.1$).

### Cost structure

- Training: two extra forward passes per paired sample (one per view).
- Inference: identical to a standard LLM. The JEPA head is discarded; only $p_\theta(x_t \mid x_{<t})$ is used.

### Why it works (intuition)

The JEPA term pulls hidden states of *semantically equivalent but surface-different* inputs together in embedding space, regularizing the representation toward meaning rather than surface form. This complements NTP, which only supervises surface tokens.

## 7. Unified view

All JEPA variants instantiate the same template:

$$
\mathcal{L} = \underbrace{ \left\| g_\phi(f_\theta(x_c),\, z) - \mathrm{sg}\!\left[ f_{\bar\theta}(x_t) \right] \right\|_2^2 }_{\text{latent prediction}} + \underbrace{ \lambda\, \mathcal{R}\!\left( \{ s_i \} \right) }_{\text{anti-collapse}}
$$

Variants differ in:

| Variant    | $x_c, x_t$                 | Target encoder $\bar\theta$ | Anti-collapse $\mathcal{R}$  |
| ---------- | -------------------------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| I-JEPA     | image patch blocks         | EMA of $\theta$             | EMA + stop-grad              |
| V-JEPA(-2) | video tubelets (+ actions) | EMA of $\theta$             | EMA + stop-grad              |
| VL-JEPA    | image vs text              | frozen text encoder         | asymmetric + frozen $Y$      |
| LeJEPA     | same as I/V-JEPA           | shared $\theta$             | SIGReg ($\mathcal{N}(0, I)$) |
| LLM-JEPA   | paired text views          | shared $\theta$ (LLM)       | stop-grad + NTP co-training  |

The core bet across all of them: **predict in representation space, not input space.**
